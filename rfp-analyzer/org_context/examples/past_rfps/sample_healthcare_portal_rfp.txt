SECTION 1: INTRODUCTION
================================================================================

1.1 Purpose
HealthFirst Medical Center is seeking qualified vendors to design, develop, and implement a comprehensive patient portal solution that will enhance patient engagement, improve access to health information, and streamline communication between patients and healthcare providers.

1.2 About HealthFirst Medical Center
HealthFirst Medical Center is a 500-bed acute care hospital serving a diverse patient population of over 250,000 patients annually. We are committed to providing high-quality, patient-centered care and leveraging technology to improve patient outcomes and satisfaction.

1.3 Project Background
Our current patient portal has limited functionality and poor user adoption (less than 30%). We need a modern, user-friendly solution that meets current patient expectations and supports our strategic goals for digital health transformation.

SECTION 2: SCOPE OF WORK
================================================================================

2.1 Functional Requirements

2.1.1 Patient Access and Authentication
- The system SHALL provide secure patient authentication using multi-factor authentication
- The system SHALL support single sign-on (SSO) integration with our existing identity provider
- The system SHALL allow patients to create accounts using email or mobile phone verification
- The system SHALL implement password policies compliant with NIST 800-63B guidelines
- The system SHALL support biometric authentication for mobile devices

2.1.2 Health Information Access
- The system SHALL provide patients with access to their complete medical records
- The system SHALL display lab results with normal ranges and trend analysis
- The system SHALL show medication lists with dosage instructions and refill status
- The system SHALL provide access to radiology reports and images
- The system SHALL display immunization history
- The system SHALL show allergy and problem lists
- The system SHALL provide visit summaries and discharge instructions
- The system SHALL support downloading health records in C-CDA format

2.1.3 Appointment Management
- The system SHALL allow patients to schedule appointments with available providers
- The system SHALL support appointment rescheduling and cancellation
- The system SHALL send appointment reminders via email and SMS
- The system SHALL display upcoming appointments with provider information
- The system SHALL integrate with our existing scheduling system (Epic)
- The system SHALL support virtual visit scheduling for telehealth appointments

2.1.4 Secure Messaging
- The system SHALL provide secure messaging between patients and care teams
- The system SHALL support message threading and conversation history
- The system SHALL allow attachment of images and documents
- The system SHALL notify patients of new messages via email/SMS
- The system SHALL support automated responses for common inquiries
- The system SHALL integrate with our EHR messaging system

2.1.5 Prescription Management
- The system SHALL allow patients to request prescription refills
- The system SHALL display current and past medications
- The system SHALL show pharmacy information and allow pharmacy changes
- The system SHALL integrate with e-prescribing systems
- The system SHALL provide medication education materials

2.1.6 Bill Pay and Financial Information
- The system SHALL display current account balances and payment history
- The system SHALL support online bill payment via credit card and ACH
- The system SHALL provide itemized billing statements
- The system SHALL support payment plans and financial assistance applications
- The system SHALL integrate with our revenue cycle management system

2.1.7 Health and Wellness Tools
- The system SHOULD provide health risk assessments
- The system SHOULD support patient-reported outcomes tracking
- The system SHOULD offer educational content library
- The system SHOULD integrate with wearable devices and health apps
- The system SHOULD provide medication adherence tracking

2.1.8 Family Access
- The system SHALL support proxy access for caregivers and family members
- The system SHALL allow parents to access minor children's records
- The system SHALL provide granular permission controls for proxy users
- The system SHALL maintain audit logs of proxy access

2.2 Non-Functional Requirements

2.2.1 Performance
- The system SHALL load pages within 2 seconds under normal load
- The system SHALL support 10,000 concurrent users
- The system SHALL handle 1 million page views per day
- The system SHALL have API response times under 200ms for 95% of requests

2.2.2 Availability
- The system SHALL maintain 99.9% uptime (excluding planned maintenance)
- Planned maintenance SHALL be limited to 4 hours per month
- The system SHALL support disaster recovery with RTO of 4 hours and RPO of 1 hour

2.2.3 Security
- The system SHALL be HIPAA compliant
- The system SHALL encrypt all data at rest using AES-256
- The system SHALL encrypt all data in transit using TLS 1.3
- The system SHALL implement role-based access control
- The system SHALL maintain comprehensive audit logs for all PHI access
- The system SHALL undergo annual penetration testing
- The system SHALL support automatic session timeout after 15 minutes of inactivity

2.2.4 Scalability
- The system SHALL support horizontal scaling to handle increased load
- The system SHALL be cloud-native and support auto-scaling
- The system SHALL handle 50% growth in user base without performance degradation

2.2.5 Usability
- The system SHALL achieve a System Usability Scale (SUS) score of 80 or higher
- The system SHALL be accessible and comply with WCAG 2.1 Level AA standards
- The system SHALL support multiple languages (English and Spanish required)
- The system SHALL be responsive and work on desktop, tablet, and mobile devices

2.2.6 Integration
- The system SHALL integrate with Epic EHR via HL7 FHIR R4 APIs
- The system SHALL support real-time data synchronization
- The system SHALL integrate with our identity provider (Okta)
- The system SHALL integrate with our payment processor
- The system SHALL provide RESTful APIs for future integrations

2.3 Compliance Requirements

2.3.1 HIPAA Compliance
- The vendor SHALL sign a Business Associate Agreement (BAA)
- The system SHALL implement all required HIPAA safeguards
- The vendor SHALL provide evidence of HIPAA compliance program
- The vendor SHALL conduct annual HIPAA risk assessments

2.3.2 Regulatory Compliance
- The system SHALL comply with 21st Century Cures Act information blocking provisions
- The system SHALL support ONC certification requirements
- The system SHALL comply with state privacy laws

2.3.3 Standards Compliance
- The system SHALL support HL7 FHIR R4 standard
- The system SHALL support C-CDA for document exchange
- The system SHALL support SMART on FHIR for app integration

SECTION 3: TECHNICAL REQUIREMENTS
================================================================================

3.1 Architecture
- The solution SHOULD be cloud-based (AWS or Azure preferred)
- The solution SHALL use microservices architecture
- The solution SHALL implement API-first design
- The solution SHALL support containerization (Docker/Kubernetes)

3.2 Technology Stack
- Frontend: Modern JavaScript framework (React, Angular, or Vue.js)
- Backend: RESTful APIs with appropriate technology stack
- Database: HIPAA-compliant database solution
- Caching: Redis or similar for performance optimization
- Message Queue: For asynchronous processing

3.3 Mobile Applications
- Native iOS app (iOS 14+)
- Native Android app (Android 10+)
- Feature parity with web portal
- Offline capability for viewing downloaded records

3.4 Development Practices
- Agile/Scrum methodology
- Continuous Integration/Continuous Deployment (CI/CD)
- Automated testing (unit, integration, end-to-end)
- Code review process
- Version control (Git)
